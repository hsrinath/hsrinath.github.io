About Me

I am a second year undergraduate student at Cornell University, majoring in Biological Sciences and concentrating in Molecular and Cell Biology. I study cell size patterning in the sepal of the Arabidopsis Thaliana in the Roeder Lab in the Weill Institute for Molecular and Cell Biology. I am a study group leader for the class BioMG 1350 Intro to Cell and Developmental Biology through the Biology Scholars Program, a selective program aimed at helping underrepresented minoritites pursue higher education in Biology. I am Vice President of Fundraising Outreach for the global health organization Unite for Sight, which fundraises for sight-restoring cataract surgeries for people living in poverty and without access to medical care in India, Ghana, and Honduras.
